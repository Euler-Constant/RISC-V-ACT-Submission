Personal insights gained during the exercise:
- UART 7E1 refers to a specific configuration of the UART protocol where data is transmitted with 7 data bits, even parity, and 1 stop bit. This configuration is commonly used in scenarios such as connecting older serial devices, like printers or modems, where compatibility with legacy systems is required, or in embedded systems where memory and bandwidth are limited.
- UART 8N2 is commonly used in scenarios where a device requires two stop bits for communication, such as interfacing with certain industrial controllers or legacy systems. This configuration can help ensure compatibility with devices that expect this specific serial communication format.
- UART 8E1 is commonly used in scenarios where data integrity is crucial, such as in industrial automation systems, communication with sensors, and energy meters that require even parity for error checking. It is also suitable for applications where devices need to communicate reliably over serial connections, ensuring that data is transmitted accurately.
- UART with 8 data bits, even parity, and 2 stop bits (8E2) is commonly used in industrial and automotive applications where reliable data transmission is critical, such as in CAN bus systems or certain legacy protocols. It provides an extra layer of error checking with parity and additional stop bits for better synchronization.
